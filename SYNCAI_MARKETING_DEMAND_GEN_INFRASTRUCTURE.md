*Prepared: 2026-02-23 | Primary ICP: Mid-Market Food & Beverage Manufacturing (Ontario/Quebec)*

## Objectives & Targets

### Business targets (given)
- **Y1:** 10–15 customers
- **Y5:** 730 customers (multi-vertical expansion assumed by Y3+)

### Operating assumptions (aligning to research/deck)
- **Sales cycle:** 45–75 days (F&B), 60–90 (T&L, discrete)
- **Typical ACV:** $18K–$30K (F&B); higher with expansion
- **ROI messaging:** 2,000–5,000% with payback <6 months

### Funnel math (to make Y1 predictable)
Use conservative mid-market benchmarks until SyncAI proves better:
- **SQL→Close:** 25–35% (target 30%)
- **MQL→SQL:** 10–20% (target 15%)
- **Lead→MQL:** 2–5% (target 3%)

**Y1 requirement (15 customers):**
- Deals won: 15
- SQLs needed: ~50 (at 30% win rate)
- MQLs needed: ~330 (at 15% MQL→SQL)

# 1) Brand & Positioning

## 1.1 Positioning statement
**For** mid-market food & beverage manufacturers in Ontario/Quebec
**who** lose margin to unplanned downtime, waste, overtime, and audit/compliance effort,
**SyncAI** is an AI-native predictive maintenance platform
**that** deploys quickly (weeks), works with existing sensors/PLCs, and delivers measurable ROI within months,
**unlike** enterprise EAM suites that are expensive/slow and basic CMMS tools that only schedule maintenance.

## 1.2 Category narrative
**“Predictive maintenance built for the mid-market.”**
- Not “AI platform” (too abstract) and not “CMMS” (too small).
- Anchor: **Operational uptime + compliance + ROI proof**.

## 1.3 Message pillars (use everywhere)
1. **Stop unplanned downtime (predict failures 7–30 days ahead)**
   - “Prevent breakdowns before peak season.”
2. **Make maintenance teams more effective (labor shortage reality)**
   - “Your best tech’s instincts—captured and scaled.”
3. **Be audit-ready without the admin burden (CFIA/HACCP)**
   - “Automatic logs and reporting, not spreadsheets.”
4. **Fast time-to-value (30–60 days)**
   - “First prevented failure in ~37 days.”
5. **Mid-market practical (price + simplicity)**
   - “Enterprise intelligence without enterprise complexity.”

## 1.4 Competitive positioning (talk track)
- **vs IBM/GE/Siemens:** 10–20× cheaper, 5× faster deployment, no data science team required.
- **vs CMMS (Fiix/UpKeep/Limble):** complements CMMS; adds predictive intelligence + optimization + ROI tracking.
- **vs hardware-first (e.g., Augury):** software-first; no proprietary hardware lock-in.

## 1.5 Personas (primary ICP)

### Persona A: Director of Operations / Plant Manager (Champion)
- **KPIs:** OEE, throughput, downtime %, waste, OT hours, on-time delivery.
- **Top fears:** peak-season failure, missing customer commitments, being “always reactive.”
- **What converts:** downtime-cost framing + proof + “minimal lift” pilot.
- **One-liner:** “Give me fewer surprises and a plan I can trust.”

### Persona B: Maintenance Manager (Power user / technical influencer)
- **KPIs:** MTBF, MTTR, PM compliance, wrench time, emergency work orders.
- **Top fears:** false alarms, extra admin, “another dashboard.”
- **What converts:** accuracy + actionable alerts + integration with current workflow.
- **One-liner:** “Don’t make my team chase noise.”

### Persona C: CFO / Owner (Economic buyer)
- **KPIs:** margin, cash, capex/opex discipline, risk.
- **Top fears:** paying for shelfware, implementation drag, unclear ROI.
- **What converts:** ROI calculator + documented savings + contract structure (pilot → annual).
- **One-liner:** “Show me the numbers and de-risk it.”

### Persona D: QA / Compliance Lead (Influencer in F&B)
- **KPIs:** audit readiness, non-conformance rate, documentation completeness.
- **Top fears:** audit failures, missing logs, uncontrolled process drift.
- **What converts:** automated reporting + traceability.

## 1.6 Proof assets & templates

### Case study template (1 page)
1. **Customer:** sub-vertical, size, location, # assets
2. **Before:** downtime %, breakdown frequency, compliance/admin burden
3. **Trigger:** major breakdown / audit finding / peak season risk
4. **Deployment:** data sources, monitored assets, time-to-value
5. **Results:** $ saved, downtime reduction, failures prevented, payback
6. **Quote:** ops leader + maintenance manager
7. **“How we did it”** (3 bullets) + CTA (ROI assessment / pilot)

### ROI calculator structure (for web + outbound)
Inputs:
- Downtime cost/hour
- Hours downtime/month
- # critical assets
- OT hours/month
- Scrap/waste $/month (optional)
Outputs:
- Annual savings range
- Payback period
- “Cost of 1 preventable failure vs annual subscription”

## 3.3 Outbound playbooks (F&B)

### Target accounts (Ontario/Quebec)
Firmographic filters:
- 100–500 employees, $15M–$150M revenue
- Multi-shift plant, 20–100 critical assets
- Sub-verticals: dairy, bakery, beverages, frozen, meat processing, packaged foods

Contacts to reach:
- Director/VP Operations, Plant Manager
- Maintenance Manager
- CFO/Controller (later-stage)
- QA/Compliance manager (situational)

#### Sequence A: “Peak Season Reliability” (Ops-first)
1. LinkedIn connect + note: peak season downtime framing
2. Email 1: “Cost of one preventable breakdown” + 15-min ROI assessment
3. Email 2: 3 failure modes relevant to their sub-vertical
4. Call 1: book assessment
5. Email 3: case-style mini story + payback math
6. Call 2 + breakup email

# 4) Growth Infrastructure

## 4.1 Tech stack (Y1 lean → Y3 scalable)

### Core (Y1)
- **CRM:** HubSpot CRM (or Salesforce if already chosen)
- **Marketing automation:** HubSpot Marketing Starter/Pro
- **Sales engagement:** Apollo or HubSpot Sequences
- **Website:** Webflow (fast iteration) or Next.js (if engineering-led)
- **Analytics:** GA4 + Google Tag Manager
- **Attribution:** HubSpot + UTMs (start simple)
- **Scheduling:** Chili Piper or HubSpot Meetings
- **Data enrichment:** Apollo/Clearbit equivalent
- **BI (optional):** Looker Studio

### Scale additions (Y2–Y3)
- **Product analytics:** PostHog/Amplitude (activation + retention)
- **Attribution:** HockeyStack/Dreamdata (multi-touch)
- **Intent:** LinkedIn ads engagement + G2 intent (later)
- **ABM:** 6sense/Demandbase (only when pipeline + content mature)

## 4.2 Tracking & governance (don’t skip)
- Enforce UTM standards (source/medium/campaign/content)
- Define lifecycle stages: Lead → MQL → SQL → Opp → Won
- One source of truth: CRM
- Monthly data QA: duplicate cleanup, stage definitions, mandatory fields

## 4.3 KPI system (what to review weekly/monthly)

### Weekly (execution)
- Outbound: new contacts, reply rate, meeting rate
- Pipeline: SQLs created, pipeline $, stage conversion
- Website: conversion rate to assessment/demo
- Content: top pages, CTA CTR

### Monthly (efficiency)
- CAC (blended + by channel)
- Payback (forecast)
- Win rate by persona + vertical
- Sales cycle by segment
- CPL/CPMQL/CP SQL (where applicable)

### North-star metrics
- **Pipeline coverage:** 3–4× next-quarter bookings target
- **Sales velocity:** opps × win rate × ACV / sales cycle
- **Net revenue retention:** target 110%+

## 4.4 Budget allocation guidance
(Percentages; set dollars based on runway)

### Y1 (prove repeatability)
- 35% people (founder time/SDR contractor/part-time marketer)
- 25% events + travel (Ontario/Quebec density)
- 20% tools + data
- 20% content production (case studies, ROI calculator, lead magnets)

### Y3–Y5 (scale)
- 45% paid acquisition + retargeting (once LTV:CAC proven)
- 25% partners/channel enablement
- 20% content/SEO + community
- 10% experiments (new verticals, new geos)

## 4.5 Operating cadence (single page)
- **Weekly growth standup:** pipeline + experiments + blockers
- **Biweekly content sprint:** 2-week backlog, ship on schedule
- **Monthly funnel review:** conversion + CAC + learnings
- **Quarterly positioning refresh:** objections, competitor moves, proof assets
