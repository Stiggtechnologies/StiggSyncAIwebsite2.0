## 1) What to Expect at Your First Chiropractic Visit (Edmonton)
**Slug:** `first-chiropractic-visit-edmonton`

### Intro
If you’ve never seen a chiropractor before, it’s normal to wonder what will happen — and whether it will feel safe, clear, and worth it. At Alberta Injury Management (AIM), our chiropractic care is **evidence-informed** and **goal-driven**. That means we assess what’s causing your symptoms, choose the right tools for your body and comfort level, and build a plan you can follow.

### What happens in your first visit
**1) A focused conversation**
We’ll ask about:
- What hurts and when it started
- What makes symptoms better or worse
- Work demands, sport demands, and sleep limitations
- Previous injuries, surgeries, or relevant health history

**2) A physical assessment**
Depending on your issue, we may assess:
- Spine and joint mobility
- Strength, balance, and coordination
- Posture and movement patterns
- Neurological screening if indicated

**3) Treatment (only if appropriate)**
If it makes sense to start care on day one, treatment may include:
- Joint mobilization or manipulation (only when appropriate and with your consent)
- Soft tissue work
- Movement and mobility exercises
- Guidance on activity modification (what to avoid, what to do)

**4) A clear plan**
You’ll leave knowing:
- What we think is driving symptoms
- What the next 2–4 weeks looks like
- What home exercises to do
- What progress should look like (objective markers)

### Do you need X-rays?
Most people do **not** need imaging before a first chiropractic visit. If there are red flags or unusual findings, we’ll help you coordinate the right next steps.

### How many visits will you need?
It depends on your symptoms, goals, and how your body responds. We plan in short blocks, reassess, and adjust. No endless schedules.

## 2) Postpartum Pelvic Floor Recovery Guide (Practical + No Shame)
**Slug:** `postpartum-pelvic-floor-recovery-guide`

### Intro
Leaking, heaviness, or pelvic discomfort after pregnancy is common — but that doesn’t mean it’s something you have to live with. Pelvic floor therapy is **private**, **respectful**, and focused on practical improvements.

### What’s normal postpartum?
Some symptoms can be common early on, but persistent symptoms deserve attention:
- Leakage when coughing/sneezing/lifting
- Pelvic heaviness/pressure
- Pain in the pelvis, tailbone, hips, or low back
- Pain with intercourse
- Difficulty returning to exercise

### What pelvic floor therapy focuses on
At AIM, postpartum pelvic health often includes:
- Breathing and pressure management (often the missing link)
- Pelvic floor muscle coordination (not just “strength”)
- Hip/core strength and movement strategy
- Return-to-exercise planning (walking → lifting → running)

### Do you have to do an internal exam?
No. Internal assessment is optional and always consent-based. Many treatment plans start with education, breathing, and movement assessment.

### When to book
Many people book around 6–12 weeks postpartum, but you can book earlier if symptoms are significant or you want help planning your return to activity.

## 3) Men’s Pelvic Health: What You Need to Know (Discreet + Straightforward)
**Slug:** `mens-pelvic-health-therapy-edmonton`

### Intro
Men’s pelvic health issues are common — they’re just not talked about enough. If you’re dealing with pelvic pain, bladder symptoms, or recovery after prostate surgery, pelvic floor therapy can be a practical, professional option.

### Common reasons men book pelvic floor therapy
- Post-prostate surgery recovery (bladder control)
- Urgency/frequency
- Pelvic or groin pain
- Tailbone pain
- Chronic pelvic pain / pelvic tension
- Return-to-exercise with improved pressure control

### What therapy looks like
A men’s pelvic health plan may include:
- Education and symptom mapping
- Breathing/pressure management
- Hip/core strengthening
- Pelvic floor coordination exercises
- Mobility and movement coaching

### Is it private?
Yes. Pelvic health appointments are confidential and consent-based.
