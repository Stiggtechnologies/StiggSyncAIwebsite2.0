# LinkedIn 7-touch Sequence — ON/QC Food & Beverage

**Goal:** Book a 15–20 min “Downtime ROI sanity check” with Ops leader.

## Touch 1 — Profile view + follow (Day 1)
No message. Make sure profile headline is outcome-based:
> “Reduce unplanned downtime 30–40% for mid-market food manufacturers (ON/QC)”

## Touch 2 — Connect request (Day 2)
**Note (<= 250 chars):**
> Hi {{FirstName}} — I work with ON/QC food manufacturers to reduce unplanned downtime (often 30–40%) without ripping out existing systems. Open to connecting?

## Touch 3 — After accept: thanks + context (Day 3)
> Thanks {{FirstName}}. Quick question — is unplanned downtime a top 3 focus this quarter at {{Company}} (especially on packaging/refrigeration)?

## Touch 4 — Share a 30-sec insight (Day 5)
> One pattern we see in food plants: the *same 5–10 assets* create most emergency work (motors/bearings, compressors, pumps). Do you have a “usual suspects” list in your plant?

## Touch 5 — Offer ROI check (Day 7)
> If you’re open, I can run a quick ROI sanity check: downtime $/hr × incidents/year → what a 30–35% reduction would mean. Worth 15 min next week?

## Touch 6 — Pilot framing (Day 10)
> Typical starting point is a 60-day pilot on 5–10 critical assets. If we don’t show measurable value, we don’t move forward. Would it be crazy to explore that for {{Company}}?

## Touch 7 — Breakup (Day 14)
> I might be off-base. Should I (a) reach out to your maintenance lead instead, or (b) circle back in 90 days?

## Optional add-on: maintenance champion version
Use the same sequence but replace the ROI language with:
- early warning (7–30 days)
- fewer emergency call-outs
